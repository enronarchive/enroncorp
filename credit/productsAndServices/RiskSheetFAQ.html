Enron Credit RiskSheet
Frequently Asked Questions

 

What is the Enron Credit RiskSheet?

With the Enron Credit RiskSheet, you can have the portfolio(s) you build on www.enroncredit.com e-mailed to you in the form of an Excel spreadsheet on a daily or weekly basis.

What data will my RiskSheet contain?

Using our web site at www.enroncredit.com you will be able to create your own portfolios, containing reference entities specifically of interest to you.

After you set up your portfolio, you can define the data you wish to receive directly on our website. The RiskSheet configuration page can be accessed through the Portfolio section of our site, under "Customize Portfolio" step 3.

 

Can I easily change the reference entities in my portfolios?

Because the RiskSheet is dispatched based on portfolios that you maintain, you can change the reference entities at any time. Simply update company names within the "Customize Portfolio" section of the website, and the changes will be reflected in your next regularly scheduled delivery.

How often will I receive my Enron Credit RiskSheet?

You can configure the system to deliver your data daily or weekly. The delivery frequency can be changed on the RiskSheet configuration page, which can be found in the Portfolio section under "Customize Portfolio."

How will I take delivery of the Enron Credit RiskSheet?

The RiskSheet will be formatted as an Excel spreadsheet and will be sent as an e-mail attachment to the address listed on your Enron Credit membership account. If you would like to change the e-mail address where your RiskSheet is sent, please e-mail us at credit-desk@enron.com and we will update your account details. You can also change your account details directly on our website.

I do not have Microsoft Excel. Can I still subscribe to the RiskSheet?

The Risk Sheet will be formatted as a Microsoft Excel 5.0 spreadsheet. If your software package is compatible with Microsoft Excel version 5.0 or higher, you should be able to take delivery of the RiskSheet. If your software is not compatible with Excel 5.0, please contact Enron Credit and we will try to find an appropriate solution.

Does the RiskSheet have any size limitations?

Since the Enron Credit RiskSheet is delivered as an e-mail attachment, the maximum number of portfolios we can support is five. Each portfolio can contain a maximum of 250 reference entities.
