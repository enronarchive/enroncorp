Wessex Water, United Kingdom

Regarded by the United Kingdom industry regulator as the most efficiently operated water and Wastewater Company in England and Wales, Wessex was acquired by Azurix in October 1998. Its high quality core management team has been in place since United Kingdom water privatization in 1989, providing water supply and wastewater treatment services in the southwest of England.

Wessex Water supplies approximately 400 million liters (106 million United States gallons) of water per day to a population of approximately 1.1 million and an average of 484 million liters (127 million United States gallons) of wastewater per day from a population of approximately 2.5 million. The company also maintains 11,000 kilometers (6,800 miles) of water mains and oversees 15,000 kilometers (9,300 miles) of wastewater pipelines.

Wessex Water is committed to providing customers with excellent service, high quality standards, reliability and value in an environmentally sensitive way. Wessex Water uses advanced treatment techniques, automation and state-of-the-art control and monitoring systems that ensure consistently high standards at low cost. Wessex Water was the first company in the United Kingdom to use membrane technology, UV disinfection and fully enclosed underground plants in its wastewater operations.

Wessex Water's operating standards emphasize a concern for detail, regardless of size or scope:

  • Automatic monitoring systems that assure high safety standards for drinking water
  • Regular and random water sampling throughout the distribution network
  • Water sample analysis, which has a pass rate of virtually 100 percent
  • Laboratory facilities that meet European accreditation standards
  • Organic farming incentives that provide innovative and unique schemes for farming fertilization needs while protecting underground aquifers
  • Pioneering procedures for the thermal drying of biosolids, which provide for the safe return of waste products back to the environment
  • The company's sustainability report, "Striking the Balance," is published annually. Wessex Water is also a founder member of the sustainable development charity, "Forum for the Future."
