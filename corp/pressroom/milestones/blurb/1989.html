The precursor to today's wholesale trading business in North America and Europe, GasBank, is launched and Enron begins trading natural gas commodities. After the deregulation of the U.S. natural gas industry, the market has to revise its methods of contracting for gas in the wholesale market. GasBank allows producers and wholesale buyers to purchase firm gas supplies and hedge the price risk of the new spot market at the same time. Today, Enron is the largest natural gas merchant in North America and the U.K.
